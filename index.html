Background

Cellular respiration is the process by which cells break down glucose to create energy in the form of ATP (adenosine triphosphate). The equation for cellular respiration is as follows:

Glucose + Water → Carbon Dioxide + Water + ATP

One can change the rate of cellular respiration by changing the amount of available glucose to be used in the reaction.

Materials and Methods

Experimental Design Note

Although we don't have the tools at home to measure the amount of ATP produced, we can measure the rate of reaction by measuring another product of the cellular respiration reaction → carbon dioxide. In this experiment, we will capture carbon dioxide by using balloons.

Materials

Warm Water
Active Dry Yeast
3 types of cereal with different sugar contents
Funnel (can be made from a piece of paper)
3 Balloons
3 plastic soda bottles
3 plastic bags
Timer

Protocol

  1. Record the sugar content of each cereal in the data table below
  2. Choose a bottle for each cereal, and label them accordingly
  3. In separate plastic bags - Mash up each cereal into a coarse dust
    Note: This step is to make it easier for us to put the cereal into the bottles. It doesn't matter how fine the cereal dust is, as long as all 3 cereals are mushed up to the same consistency
  4. In each bottle place the following things. Use funnel to make sure:
    • 2 tsp yeast
    • ¼ cup respective cereal
  5. As quickly as possible, add ½ cup warm water to each bottle, and cover with a balloon
  6. Record which balloon stands up 1st, 2nd, and 3rd
